**Headline:** Why a PhD in Design built an AI orchestrator based on Gardner's Theory of Multiple Intelligences

---

A few weeks ago, a question started following me around:

*What if, instead of asking a single AI model about a phenomenon, I could ask 9 different "specialists" — each trained to see the world through a unique cognitive lens — and then synthesize everything into a new concept?*

Not a summary. A concept that only exists because 9 different perspectives collided.

I spent years studying Design, I teach about cognition and games, and I've always been fascinated by Howard Gardner's Theory of Multiple Intelligences. The idea that there isn't one single form of intelligence — but several (linguistic, logical-mathematical, spatial, musical, interpersonal, intrapersonal, bodily-kinesthetic, naturalistic, existential) — has always resonated with me.

So I built it: **Hbrain**.

It's an open-source multi-agent orchestrator that:

⚡ Fires the same question to 9 parallel AI agents
🧠 Each one interprets the question through a different intelligence (with its own personality defined in a `soul.md`)
🔄 Collects all responses
🪄 **Synthesizes a NEW concept** — not a collage, but an integrated understanding

I asked Hbrain "what is a synapse?" The **linguistic** agent traced the etymology (*syn-haptein* = "to grasp together") and described the synaptic cleft as a comma in the body's discourse. The **spatial** agent drew the micro-architecture — vesicles as cargo ships, receptors as docking bays. The **musical** agent heard polyrhythm in temporal summation. The **existential** agent sat with the question of how matter becomes meaning in that 20nm gap.

The synthesis that emerged:

> *"The synapse is not the point of contact between neurons — it is the interval that makes connection meaningful. It is the active void where continuity is deliberately fractured so the signal gains depth, ambiguity, and creative power."*

This didn't come from a single model. It came from the collision of 9 perspectives.

The project is 100% open source (MIT), on GitHub, ~500 lines of Python with asyncio. It works with any OpenAI-compatible API. I've been using deepseek-v4-flash (~$0.001 per run).

🔗 https://github.com/tiagovficagna/Hbrain
